# Basics

## General
- No of genes discovered  $\rightarrow$ 20,000
- %age of genes coding prteins  $\rightarrow$ 1.5%
- Chromosomal disorders are due to
	- Euploidy (multiple of n)
	- Aneuploidy (Not a multiple of n  $\rightarrow$ 2n +1, etc)
	- Structural Defect

### Subtype of chromosomes
- Based on centromere
	- Metacentric
		- Centromere present in the ceter
		- Example - **1,3,16,19,20**
	- Submetacentric
		- Centromere present slightly on a side
		- Example - All except the lists
	- Acro-centric
		- Centromere towards one end
		- Example - **Y, 13, 14, 15, 21, 22**
	- Telocentric
		- Centromere present at tip
		- Not present in humans
## Karyotyping
- Study of chromosomes
- Samples
	- Amniotic Cells
	- Skin fibroblasts
	- Epithelial cells of buccal mucosa
	- Peripheral blood lymphocytes
- Cholchicine  $\rightarrow$ Metaphase arrest
- Autosomes are arranged depending on length in aascending order
- Chromosomes have short arm **q** and long arm **p**
- <u>Carnoy's Fixative</u> is used
	- Methanol : Glacial Acetic Acid  $\rightarrow$ 3:1
- G banding  $\rightarrow$ MC banding patters

## Genetic Disorders Common Causes
- $\uparrow$ Maternal Age
	- Down's Syndrome
- $\uparrow$ Parental Age
	- Marfan Syndrome
	- Osteogenesis Imperfecta
	- Neurofibromatosis
	- Achondroplasia
- 22q11 deletion  $\rightarrow$ DiGeorge Syndrome
	- Thymus/Parathyroid gland dysfunction
	- Abnormal facies
	- Congenital Cardiac Defects
	- $\uparrow$ risk of schizophrenia
- 5p deletion - **Cri du chat** Syndrome
	- Strange cry
	- Developmental abnormalities
	- Colobama in eyes
